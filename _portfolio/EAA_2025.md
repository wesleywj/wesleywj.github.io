excerpt: 'This study investigates how female employment and family formation trajectories jointly intersect with welfare regimes to shape later life health outcomes. Using a machine learning-based g-computation framework, it estimates the effects of typical employment and family trajectories across Scandinavian, Mediterranean, and Corporatist welfare regimes. The results show that health advantages and disadvantages are shaped less by roles themselves than by the institutional contexts in which women combine and sustain them across the life course.'
